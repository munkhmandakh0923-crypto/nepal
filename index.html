漢字 Kanji
Essential kanji for construction work — with readings, English meaning, and Nepali
What is Kanji? Japanese uses 3 types of writing: ひらがな (hiragana), カタカナ (katakana), and 漢字 (kanji). Kanji are Chinese-origin characters where each character carries a meaning. The kanji below appear frequently in carpentry work.

काञ्जी के हो? जापानीमा तीन प्रकारका लिपि हुन्छन् — हिरागाना, काताकाना र काञ्जी। काञ्जी चिनियाँ मूलका अक्षर हुन् र प्रत्येक अक्षरको आफ्नै अर्थ हुन्छ।
ひらがな Hiragana
The basic Japanese alphabet — 46 characters. You must memorize all of these!
Why is hiragana important? Hiragana is used for verb endings, particles, and connecting words. For example: 切る、切って、切らない — the red parts are all hiragana. Without knowing these, you cannot conjugate verbs!

हिरागाना किन महत्त्वपूर्ण छ? हिरागाना क्रियाका अन्त्य, कारक चिह्न र जोड्ने शब्दहरूमा प्रयोग हुन्छ। यी नजानीकन क्रिया रूपान्तरण गर्न सकिँदैन!

🔑 て-form Rules — Most Used on the Job Site
U-verb changes: く→いて ｜ ぐ→いで ｜ す→して ｜ つ/る/う→って ｜ ぬ/ぶ/む→んで
て-form uses: ～てください (please do) ｜ ～ています (doing now) ｜ ～てから (after doing) ｜ ～ておく (prepare in advance)
て रूपको प्रयोग: ～てください (गर्नुहोस्) ｜ ～ています (गर्दैछु) ｜ ～てから (गरेपछि)
助詞 Particles
Japanese particles mark the role of each word in a sentence — like case markers. Essential for correct sentences!
💡 Comparison with English / Nepali Grammar
English role Nepali (कारक) Japanese particle Function
Topic marker— (none)は・がMarks topic or subject
Direct objectलाई / -लाईをWhat the action is done TO
Direction / at / in-मा / -तिरに・へDirection, time, location of existence
From-बाट / -देखिから・よりStarting point, origin
By / with (tool)-ले / -द्वाराでTool / method / place of action
With (person)-सँगとTogether with a person
Possessive ('s)-कोのOwnership / belonging
は
wa
Topic marker / विषय चिह्न
Marks the topic of the sentence. Difference from が: は = known topic, が = new info or emphasis.
वाक्यको विषय जनाउँछ।
この木材は3メートルです。
Kono mokuzai wa san mētoru desu.
This lumber is 3 meters.
यो काठ ३ मिटर छ।
が
ga
Subject marker / कर्ता चिह्न
Marks the doer/subject. Always used with あります/います.
कर्ता जनाउँछ। आवश्यक: あります/います सँग सधैँ が।
クギがありません。
Kugi ga arimasen.
There are no nails.
कील छैन।
を
wo
Object marker / कर्म चिह्न
Marks what the action is done to (direct object).
क्रिया कसमा गरिन्छ त्यो जनाउँछ।
板を切ってください。
Ita wo kitte kudasai.
Please cut the board.
बोर्ड काट्नुहोस्।
に
ni
Direction / Time / Existence location
3 uses:
① Direction — "to/toward"
② Time — 9時に = at 9 o'clock
③ Existence location — with あります/います
तीन प्रयोग: दिशा, समय, अवस्थिति।
屋根に上ります。
Yane ni noborimasu.
I go up to the roof.
छानामा चढ्छु।
で
de
Tool / Method / Place of action
2 uses:
① Tool/method — "by means of"
② Place where action happens
⚠️ に vs で: に = exists there, で = action happens there
औजार/तरिका र क्रिया हुने स्थान।
のこぎりで木を切ります。
Nokogiri de ki wo kirimasu.
I cut wood with a saw.
आरीले काठ काट्छु।
と
to
Together with / and (list)
Used for doing something with a person, or listing things.
कसैसँग मिलेर, वा वस्तुहरू सूचीबद्ध गर्न।
職長と話します。
Shokuchō to hanashimasu.
I talk with the foreman.
फोरम्यानसँग कुरा गर्छु।
の
no
Possessive ('s) / of
Links two nouns: A の B = "B of A" or "A's B".
दुई नामलाई जोड्छ। को (Nepali: -को) जस्तै।
会社の道具です。
Kaisha no dōgu desu.
It is the company's tool.
कम्पनीको औजार हो।
から
kara
From / After doing
Marks starting point. Also used as "after doing" (て+から).
सुरुको बिन्दु। "गरेपछि" पनि भन्न सकिन्छ।
確認してから進めます。
Kakunin shite kara susumemasu.
I'll proceed after checking.
जाँचेपछि अगाडि बढ्छु।
も
mo
Also / too / even
Replaces は or が/を to add the meaning of "also/too".
"पनि / समेत" भन्न प्रयोग हुन्छ।
ヘルメットもつけてください。
Herumetto mo tsukete kudasai.
Please also wear a helmet.
हेलमेट पनि लगाउनुहोस्।
か
ka
Question marker / प्रश्न चिह्न
Added at end of sentence to make a yes/no question (like "?").
वाक्यको अन्त्यमा थपेर प्रश्न बनाइन्छ।
測りましたか？
Hakarimashita ka?
Did you measure it?
नाप्नुभयो?
⚠️ に vs で — Location particles: two different meanings!
に → Where something EXISTS
道具箱に工具があります。
Dōgu-bako ni kōgu ga arimasu.
Tools are in the toolbox.
औजारबाकसमा सामान छ।
で → Where ACTION happens
道具箱で工具を探します。
Dōgu-bako de kōgu wo sagashimasu.
I search for tools in the toolbox.
औजारबाकसमा खोज्छु।
Rule: Ask yourself — "Does it just EXIST there, or is an ACTION happening there?"

खतरा छ! रोक्नुहोस्!

खुट्टाको तलतिर ध्यान दिनुहोस्।

यहाँ प्रवेश निषेध छ।

を + した

बिजुली काटेपछि काम गर्नुहोस्।
て + から
